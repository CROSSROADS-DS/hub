<section class="page-section">
  <div class="container content">
    <h2>Project vision</h2>
    <p>Data science is relevant far beyond degree programs devoted to the field. CROSSROADS helps students encounter data science through subjects and questions they already care about, while creating opportunities to apply what they learn in real settings.</p>

    <h2>Educational approach</h2>
    <p>The project combines three mutually reinforcing components:</p>
    <ul>
      <li><strong>Domain exploration modules</strong> that connect foundational data science with different academic disciplines.</li>
      <li><strong>The Data Science Studio</strong>, where students work on authentic projects with campus and community partners.</li>
      <li><strong>Collaborative workshops and community spaces</strong> that support mutual learning among educators, students, and partners.</li>
    </ul>

    <h2>The platform</h2>
    <p>This Hub is the public entry point to the CROSSROADS Pilot. Public learning materials live in independent module repositories, while educator-only resources remain in separate private repositories. Approved content is released through a controlled publication process from one canonical development source.</p>

    <h2>From pilot to reusable framework</h2>
    <p>The CROSSROADS Pilot serves as the project's <strong>Reference Platform</strong>: a working implementation used to demonstrate, test, and refine the framework. A future <strong>Platform Starter Kit</strong> will package reusable templates, tools, and guidance so that other institutions can create their own reference platforms.</p>

    <div class="notice">
      <strong>Current status:</strong> Hub v0.1 establishes the public structure. Browser-based notebooks, automated deployment, and expanded guidance will be added in later stages.
    </div>
  </div>
</section>
